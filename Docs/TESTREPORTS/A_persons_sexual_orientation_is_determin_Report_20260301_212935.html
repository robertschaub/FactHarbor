While 11 evidence items document sexual orientation fluidity and developmental changes—particularly during adolescence—this fluidity does not support the notion that orientation is freely chosen or primarily shaped by post-natal socialization. Limitations

Additionally, the relationship between reported orientation fluidity and underlying biological substrates remains incompletely characterized, particularly regarding whether fluidity reflects measurement issues, genuine phenotypic plasticity, or changes in self-identification rather than underlying disposition.
