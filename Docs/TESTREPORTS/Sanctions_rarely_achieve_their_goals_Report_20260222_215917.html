Limitations
The analysis could not definitively resolve whether observed economic impacts translate into policy compliance, as this depends on unmeasured political will and domestic factors within target states. The evidence base lacks sufficient longitudinal data to assess long-term effectiveness beyond immediate economic disruption. Coordination quality, enforcement rigor, and circumvention dynamics—identified as critical variables—remain inadequately quantified across the historical record. The 65% confidence reflects instability in cross-boundary synthesis and the absence of consensus methodologies for measuring "success" in sanctions regimes.
