# Fact-Checking Infrastructure Quality Levels by Democratic Country

**Date:** April 6, 2026
**Sources:** IFCN Signatories Database, Duke Reporters' Lab Census 2025, EFCSN, State of Fact-Checkers Report 2025
**Purpose:** Where does fact-checking infrastructure exist, how mature is it, and where are the gaps?

---

## Global Summary

- **443** active fact-checking projects in **116** countries (Duke Reporters' Lab 2025)
- **170+** IFCN-certified signatories across **71** countries
- **61** EFCSN members (European)
- **76%** of organizations describe themselves as "vulnerable or in crisis" (2025)
- **45%** report revenue declines; grants are the only growing revenue source

### Tier 4 — Minimal Infrastructure
No IFCN presence, reliance on international organizations, no local capacity.

Most remaining democracies fall here: Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Latvia, Lithuania, Estonia, Malta, Slovenia, Luxembourg, Iceland, and many smaller or newer democracies. They rely on international coverage (AFP, Reuters, dpa, BBC) rather than domestic fact-checking organizations.

## Key Observations

### Switzerland's Position

Switzerland stands out as the **wealthiest democracy without any IFCN-certified fact-checking organization**. Despite:
- 4 national languages (DE/FR/IT/RM)
- A strong media landscape (SRF/RTS/RSI, NZZ, Tamedia, Ringier)
- Active journalism training (MAZ Luzern, Keystone-SDA Faktencheck)

...there is no dedicated, independent, systematic fact-checking operation. The closest equivalent is:
- SRF Netzwerk Faktencheck: ~15 people, part-time side function
- swissinfo.ch: occasional fact-checks on referendum claims
- dpa: covers Switzerland from Germany as part of DACH region

**This is FactHarbor's strongest market opportunity.**

### The 2025 Funding Crisis

The withdrawal of Meta (January 2025) and Google's ClaimReview reduction (June 2025) created a structural crisis:
- 76% of organizations "vulnerable or in crisis"
- 45% revenue decline
- Lead Stories had signed a new Meta contract 2 weeks before cancellation
- AFP Fact Check's 150-person operation at risk

**This creates both urgency and opportunity** for independent, open-source infrastructure like FactHarbor.

### Taiwan as a Model

Taiwan's fact-checking ecosystem is notably advanced for its size:
- 3 IFCN-certified organizations
- CoFacts: crowdsourced, LINE chatbot integration, open data
- Strong government awareness without direct control
- Advanced threat landscape (Chinese disinformation) drives innovation

**Lesson for FactHarbor:** Crowdsourced verification + chatbot integration + open data = high civic engagement model.

### AI Adoption Gap

Over 50% of IFCN organizations now use AI tools, but almost exclusively for:
- Claim detection / monitoring (Full Fact AI)
- Image/video verification (InVID, WeVerify)
- Translation and transcription

**No organization uses AI for automated verdict generation** — this remains FactHarbor's unique position.
